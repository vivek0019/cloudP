Compostable vs Biodegradable Plastics

Understanding the Difference

Compostable Plastics

Made from organic materials, compostable plastics decompose into non-toxic elements like water, carbon dioxide, and biomass in a compost environment within a specific timeframe.

Biodegradable Plastics

These plastics break down naturally, but they don't always decompose fully and may leave behind microplastics, depending on environmental conditions and the type of plastic used.

Key Difference

All compostable plastics are biodegradable, but not all biodegradable plastics are compostable. Compostable plastics must meet specific standards to decompose in composting conditions within a certain time, whereas biodegradable plastics break down at varying rates depending on their environment.

Benefits of Composting

Composting is generally faster, more controlled, and leaves no toxic residue, making it a better option for reducing plastic pollution in waste management setups.
