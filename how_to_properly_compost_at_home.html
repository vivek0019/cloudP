How to Properly Compost at Home

Steps to Start Composting

1. Choose a Composting Method

Decide on the composting method that best suits your needs: traditional composting, vermicomposting (using worms), or a compost tumbler. Each method has its benefits and can be tailored to different spaces and needs.

2. Select a Composting Bin

Choose a compost bin or tumbler that fits your space. For a traditional compost pile, you can create a simple enclosure in your garden or use a commercially available compost bin.

3. Add the Right Materials

Start by adding a mix of 'green' materials (nitrogen-rich, like vegetable scraps and coffee grounds) and 'brown' materials (carbon-rich, like dried leaves and cardboard). Aim for a ratio of about 2:1 brown to green materials.

4. Maintain Your Compost

Turn your compost regularly to aerate it and help speed up the decomposition process. Ensure that the compost remains moist but not too wet. If it’s too dry, add water; if too wet, add more brown materials.

5. Harvest the Compost

After a few months to a year, your compost should be dark, crumbly, and earthy-smelling. This means it’s ready to use in your garden to enrich the soil.
